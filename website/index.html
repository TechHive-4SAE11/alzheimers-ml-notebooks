Key Findings

  • SVM yields the fewest false negatives (20) — critical in screening where missed diagnoses delay treatment.
  • SVM's ROC curve dominates all other models across most FPR thresholds.
  • Higher false positives (47) are clinically acceptable: positives trigger confirmatory MRI.
  • All 16 Lasso-selected features genuinely informative — PCA could not reduce further.
  • Clinical priority: Recall > Precision for a screening tool.

Limitations

  • SVM is a black-box model (SHAP / LIME needed for regulatory approval).
  • Single train/test split — no cross-validation.
  • No neuroimaging or genetic markers (APOE ε4).

Key Challenges

  • Severe class imbalance: ModerateDemented has only 64 images (1%).
  • Inter-class confusion: VeryMild vs Mild dementia is visually subtle.
  • Small dataset: ~5,000 effective training images — small for deep learning.

Suggested Improvements

  • More data / synthetic generation (GANs) for rare classes.
  • Focal Loss instead of categorical cross-entropy.
  • Ensemble methods across architectures.
  • Vision Transformers (ViT).
  • Grad-CAM for clinical explainability.

Key Findings

  • 87% consensus accuracy — the system reliably reproduces clinical care-plan decisions.
  • Z-scored heatmaps reveal distinct patient archetypes (high MMSE/ADL = healthier; high MemoryComplaints = advanced disease).
  • Cluster–Diagnosis alignment confirms unsupervised segmentation captures meaningful clinical groups.
  • KMeans chosen for deployment: interpretable centroids + O(n×k×d) scalability.

Limitations

  • Cold-start problem for new care plans.
  • Equal feature weighting — domain-specific weighting could help.
  • Binary interaction signal (no patient outcome data).
  • Historical care plan bias propagation.
